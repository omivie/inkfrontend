Frequently Asked Questions

The ones we’re asked most often. If your question isn’t covered, the contact form always wins — we reply within one business day.

Cartridges & printers

Are these genuine or compatible cartridges?

Both. Every product page clearly says Genuine or Compatible and lists the manufacturer (Brother, Canon, HP, Epson, Lexmark, Samsung, Kyocera, etc.). We never blur the line between the two.

Genuine cartridges are made by the printer manufacturer. Compatibles are third-party cartridges that we’ve quality-tested ourselves and that work in printers listed as compatible.

Will a compatible cartridge void my printer warranty?

No. Under the New Zealand Consumer Guarantees Act, a printer manufacturer cannot void your warranty just because you used a third-party cartridge. They can only refuse warranty for damage that the third-party cartridge actually caused.

In practice that means: you keep your printer warranty, and if our compatible cartridge somehow damages your printer (it’s rare, but possible), we’ll cover the repair under our 12-month compatible guarantee.

How do I find the right cartridge for my printer?

Three ways:

  • Use the Printer Models tool on our home page — pick your brand and model and we’ll show every cartridge that fits.
  • Type the cartridge code on your old box (e.g. “LC233”, “CN053AA”, “TN-2350”) into the search bar at the top.
  • Email inkandtoner@windowslive.com with your printer model — we’ll send you a list with prices.
What if my printer doesn’t recognise the cartridge?

Try this first: remove the cartridge, power the printer off at the wall, wait 60 seconds, power back on, and reinsert. About 80% of “not recognised” problems are a chip-handshake glitch that this resets.

If it still won’t recognise the cartridge, email us with your printer model and the cartridge SKU on the box. We’ll either talk you through a firmware setting (some Brother and HP models need a specific menu sequence) or replace the cartridge under our compatible-cartridge guarantee.

What does “page yield” on the product page mean?

Page yield is the manufacturer’s estimate of how many pages a cartridge will print at 5% coverage (roughly a normal letter or invoice). Real-world yield depends on what you’re printing — photos, dense colour, double-sided pages all use more ink. Use the page-yield number to compare cartridges, not as a guarantee.

Do you stock typewriter and dot-matrix ribbons?

Yes — see the Ribbons section. We hold the most-asked-for typewriter ribbons (Brother, Olivetti, Olympia, Panasonic, Sharp) plus current-production printer ribbons.

Orders & shipping

How fast is delivery?

Same-day dispatch on orders placed before 2pm on a working day. Then:

  • Auckland urban: typically next working day.
  • Rest of North Island: 1–3 working days.
  • South Island: 2–4 working days.
  • Rural addresses: add 1–2 working days for the rural delivery leg.

Full table on the Shipping & Delivery Policy.

How much does shipping cost?

Free on every order of $100 or more, anywhere in NZ. Below that, urban North Island starts at $7. The exact figure for your address shows at checkout.

Do you ship outside New Zealand?

No — NZ addresses only. Returns and warranty over the Tasman or to the Pacific Islands take longer than a cartridge is worth, and we’d rather refer you to a local supplier.

Do you charge a card surcharge or “processing fee”?

No. The price you see at checkout is the price you pay. We don’t add card surcharges, handling fees, processing fees, or fuel surcharges. The total is the total.

Where do orders ship from? Do you dropship?

Yes — we’re upfront about it. We’re a small NZ team running this business from an Auckland office and we don’t hold stock ourselves. Every order is picked, packed, and dispatched directly by one of our New Zealand wholesale supplier partners through the same NZ Post / Aramex networks any local retailer uses, so transit times are the same as buying off any NZ shelf.

Most SKUs dispatch the same working day if you order before 2pm. A small number of low-velocity items add 1–2 working days; we flag this on the product page with a “Dispatched by supplier” note so there are no surprises. Customer support, returns, refunds, and warranty all run through us — never the supplier. See the Shipping & Delivery Policy for full detail.

Can I track my order?

Every order ships tracked. The tracking link arrives by email the moment the courier scans the parcel and is also visible inside your account at /account/orders.

Returns & warranty

What if my cartridge is faulty?

Email a photo of the cartridge and a one-line description of what’s wrong to inkandtoner@windowslive.com. We’ll either replace it the same day (no waiting for the faulty one to come back) or refund you in full.

The Consumer Guarantees Act gives you these rights regardless of any time limit — there’s no “30-day window” on faulty goods. See the Refund & Return Policy.

Can I return an unopened cartridge if I changed my mind?

Yes — within 30 days of delivery, with the factory seal intact and packaging undamaged, we refund the item price. You cover return shipping. No restocking fee.

Can I return an opened cartridge?

Only if it’s faulty — see “What if my cartridge is faulty?” above. Ink and toner are consumables, so we can’t take an opened cartridge back as a change-of-mind return (we’d be passing a degraded cartridge on to the next customer, which isn’t fair).

What’s the warranty on a compatible cartridge?

12 months from the order date. If a compatible cartridge fails inside that window, we replace it free.

Account & privacy

Do I need an account to order?

No. Guest checkout is fine. Signing up just means we save your delivery address, your printer list, and your favourites for next time.

What data do you collect?

Only what we need to run an ink shop: name, email, delivery address, phone, items ordered, and basic site analytics. We never sell or rent your data to anyone. Full detail in the Privacy Policy.

How do I delete my account or my data?

Email inkandtoner@windowslive.com with the subject “Privacy request”. We respond within 20 working days (usually much sooner) and delete everything we’re not legally required to keep — note Inland Revenue rules require us to retain order records for 7 years.
