Privacy Policy

Last updated: 5 April 2026

Introduction

InkCartridges.co.nz ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or make a purchase.

We comply with the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020 and the information privacy principles (IPPs) contained within it. We collect and use your personal information only for the purposes described in this policy. Where we rely on your consent (such as for marketing emails), you may withdraw that consent at any time.

Information We Collect

Personal Information You Provide

When you make a purchase, create an account, or interact with our website, we may collect:

  • Name (first and last)
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Delivery and billing addresses
  • Payment information (processed securely by our payment providers — we do not store your full card details)
  • Company name and GST number (for business accounts)
  • Contact form enquiries (name, email, phone, order number, subject, and message)
  • Newsletter subscription email address

Account Information

If you create an account, we also store:

  • Your login credentials (password is securely hashed — we cannot see it)
  • Saved delivery addresses
  • Saved printers (brand, model, and nickname)
  • Favourite products list
  • Order history
  • Email marketing preferences

If you sign in using Google OAuth, we receive your name and email address from Google. We do not receive or store your Google password.

Automatically Collected Information

When you visit our website, we automatically collect:

  • IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Device information (desktop, mobile, tablet)
  • Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • Referring website (how you found us)
  • Products viewed, searched for, and added to cart
  • Cart activity (including abandoned cart data, to help us improve our service)

How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

  • Process and fulfil your orders, including shipping and delivery
  • Communicate about your orders, account, and any issues that arise
  • Provide customer support and respond to your enquiries
  • Send marketing communications about products, offers, and promotions (only with your consent)
  • Improve our website, products, and services based on how customers use our site
  • Detect and prevent fraud, bot activity, or other illegal activities
  • Verify your email address for order processing
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our terms of service

Information Sharing and Third-Party Services

We may share your information with the following categories of service providers:

  • Shipping and courier companies (such as NZ Post and CourierPost) to deliver your orders
  • Payment processors (Stripe and PayPal) to process payments securely
  • Email service providers to send order confirmations and marketing communications (with your consent)
  • Analytics providers (Google Analytics) to help us understand website usage
  • Customer review services (Google Customer Reviews) to collect and display verified customer feedback, including post-purchase review surveys
  • Bot protection services (Cloudflare Turnstile) to protect our website from automated abuse — this service may collect device and browser information to verify you are a real visitor
  • Authentication providers (Google OAuth) if you choose to sign in with your Google account
  • Infrastructure and database providers (Supabase, hosted on AWS) to manage accounts and store data securely
  • Legal authorities when required by law, court order, or government request

We do not sell your personal information to third parties. We only share information with trusted partners who help us operate our business and serve you better, and all partners are bound by confidentiality agreements.

Overseas Data Transfers

Some of our service providers are based or store data outside New Zealand, including in the United States and other countries. These include Stripe (payments), PayPal (payments), Google (analytics, reviews, and authentication), Cloudflare (bot protection), and Supabase/AWS (infrastructure). Before sharing information with overseas parties, we take reasonable steps to ensure they protect your information to a standard comparable to the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020, in accordance with Information Privacy Principle 12.

Cookies, Local Storage, and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Remember your preferences and shopping cart contents
  • Keep you logged in to your account
  • Understand how you use our website so we can improve it
  • Protect our website from automated abuse (via Cloudflare Turnstile)
  • Measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential cookies: Required for the website to function properly (e.g., shopping cart, login sessions, bot protection). These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies: Help us understand how visitors use our site. These are disabled by default and only activated when you accept analytics cookies via our cookie consent notice.
  • Marketing cookies: Used to show you relevant advertisements (only with your consent).

Local Storage

In addition to cookies, we use your browser's local storage (localStorage and sessionStorage) to store:

  • Your shopping cart contents
  • Your cookie consent preferences
  • Session information for guest checkout
  • Recently viewed products and other preferences

This data is stored only on your device and is not transmitted to our servers unless required (e.g., cart contents when placing an order).

You can control cookies through your browser settings and clear local storage at any time. Note that disabling essential cookies or clearing local storage may affect your ability to use some features of our website, such as the shopping cart.

Data Security

We implement appropriate security measures to protect your information, including:

  • SSL/TLS encryption for all data transmission between your browser and our servers
  • Secure payment processing through PCI DSS compliant payment providers
  • Bot protection to prevent automated attacks and fraudulent activity
  • Access controls to limit who can view your information within our organisation
  • Regular security reviews and updates to our systems
  • Secure data storage with regular backups

While we take all reasonable precautions to protect your data, no method of transmission over the internet is 100% secure. We encourage you to use strong passwords and keep your account credentials confidential.

Privacy Breach Notification

In the event of a privacy breach that is likely to cause serious harm, we are required under the Privacy Act 2020 to notify the Office of the Privacy Commissioner and any affected individuals as soon as practicable. We maintain internal procedures to detect, assess, and respond to privacy breaches promptly.

Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to:

  • Provide you with our services and fulfil your orders
  • Maintain your account if you have one
  • Comply with legal obligations (such as tax records, which must be kept for 7 years)
  • Resolve disputes and enforce our agreements

If you close your account or request deletion of your data, we will delete or anonymise your personal information within a reasonable timeframe, except where we are required by law to retain it.

Your Rights Under the Privacy Act 2020

Under the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020, you have the right to:

  • Access (IPP 6): Request a copy of the personal information we hold about you. We will respond within 20 working days as required by law.
  • Correction (IPP 7): Request correction of personal information that is inaccurate, out of date, incomplete, or misleading.
  • Withdraw consent: Unsubscribe from marketing communications at any time, or withdraw consent for any processing based solely on your consent.
  • Request deletion: While deletion is not a specific statutory right under the Privacy Act 2020 (unlike GDPR), we will delete or anonymise your personal information on request where we are not required by law to retain it.
  • Complain: Lodge a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner if you believe we have breached your privacy rights.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at inkandtoner@windowslive.com. We will respond to access and correction requests within 20 working days as required by law.

Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites, such as manufacturer sites or payment providers. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of these external sites. We encourage you to read the privacy policies of any third-party sites you visit.

Children's Privacy

Our website is not intended for individuals under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children or minors. If you believe we have inadvertently collected information from someone under 18, please contact us immediately and we will take steps to delete it.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We will notify you of significant changes by:

  • Posting a notice on our website
  • Sending you an email (if you have an account with us)
  • Updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this policy

We encourage you to review this policy periodically to stay informed about how we protect your information.
