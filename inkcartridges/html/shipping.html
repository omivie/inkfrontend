Shipping & Delivery Policy

Tracked courier on every order, anywhere in New Zealand. Free over $100. Same-day dispatch on orders placed before 2pm on a working day. Below: handling time, zone-by-zone rates, transit estimates, and exactly how every order is dispatched by our New Zealand wholesale supplier partners on our behalf.

Last updated: 5 May 2026 Version: 2026.05

1. Snapshot

  • Where we ship: all of New Zealand, urban and rural, including Waiheke, Stewart Island, and the Chathams.
  • Carriers: NZ Post and Aramex (CourierPost network) — selected per parcel for the fastest realistic transit time.
  • Handling time: Same-day dispatch on orders placed before 2:00pm NZT, Monday – Friday. Orders placed after 2:00pm, on weekends, or on NZ public holidays dispatch the next working day.
  • Free shipping on every order of $100 or more, anywhere in NZ.
  • Tracking emailed automatically the moment the courier scans the parcel.

No hidden fees

The shipping price you see at checkout is the shipping price you pay. We don’t add fuel surcharges, “rural levies”, or signature fees on top of the rate shown. Rural addresses are priced into the rural column below.

2. Rates by zone

Rates are in NZD and include GST. The exact figure for your address shows at checkout once you’ve entered your delivery suburb.

3. Handling time vs. transit time

These are two separate clocks, and we’re only in control of the first one:

  • Handling time — the time between you clicking Pay and the parcel leaving our supplier partner. Same-day if you order before 2pm on a working day. After-hours, weekend, and public-holiday orders go out the next working day.
  • Transit time — the time between the courier picking up your parcel and them leaving it at your door. This is set by NZ Post / Aramex networks; we publish their target windows above as estimates.

If the courier misses their target, we chase them on your behalf — please email or call as soon as the parcel is more than one working day past estimate and we’ll open a trace and (if it doesn’t turn up) replace your order.

4. How orders are fulfilled — supplier-direct dispatch

We’re a small NZ business operating from an Auckland office. We don’t hold stock ourselves — every cartridge we sell is held by one of our New Zealand wholesale supplier partners and dispatched directly by them on our behalf. This is sometimes called “dropshipping”, and we want to be upfront about it: it’s the model that lets a small Kiwi team carry every brand and yield without big-box markups.

What this means in practice

  • You order on this site, pay us, and we receive your tracking GST receipt — the standard end-to-end ecommerce experience.
  • Behind the scenes, we route the picking and packing to the New Zealand supplier partner who holds the cartridge for your order.
  • Suppliers ship through the same NZ Post / Aramex networks any local retailer uses — there’s no extra charge, different rate, or longer transit time.
  • Most SKUs dispatch the same working day if you order before 2pm. A small number of low-velocity SKUs add 1–2 working days; the product page calls this out with a “Dispatched by supplier” note in the stock block.
  • We pre-screen every supplier partner: all are New Zealand based, hold genuine cartridges from authorised channels, and are bound by our quality terms.
  • Customer support, returns, refunds, GST receipts, and warranty all go through us — not the supplier. You only ever deal with us.

Transparency on supplier fulfilment

We don’t hold stock at our Auckland office — every order is picked, packed, and dispatched directly by one of our New Zealand wholesale supplier partners through the same NZ Post / Aramex networks any local retailer uses. Most orders dispatch the same working day; a small number of low-velocity SKUs add 1–2 working days to handling and that lead time is disclosed on the relevant product page.

5. Mixed orders and split shipments

If your order contains items held by more than one of our supplier partners, we’ll usually split the shipment so the faster-dispatch half arrives sooner. You’ll get a separate tracking number for each parcel and pay shipping only once — never twice.

6. Tracking, signatures, and delivery

  • Every order ships tracked. The tracking link arrives by email on dispatch and is also visible inside your account at /account/orders.
  • Standard delivery is no-signature-required to a safe drop point. If no one’s home and there’s no obvious safe spot, the courier leaves a calling card and re-attempts the next working day or holds at the nearest depot.
  • You can request signature-required delivery in the order notes — no extra charge, but it does add a day if you’re out when the courier calls.
  • PO Boxes are accepted via NZ Post; couriers can’t deliver to a PO Box, so we automatically choose the right carrier for the address you give us.

7. Cut-offs and public holidays

2pm NZT, Monday – Friday, is the daily cut-off for same-day dispatch. Orders received after 2pm dispatch the next working day. We don’t dispatch on Saturday, Sunday, or NZ public holidays — Auckland Anniversary Day and Wellington/regional anniversary days follow the local courier network. The two longest pauses each year are Christmas Day to 2 January and the Easter weekend.

8. International shipping

We ship to New Zealand addresses only. The most common reason customers ask: returns from Australia or the Pacific Islands take longer than the cartridge is worth, and we’d rather refer you to a local supplier than send a parcel that may sit in customs.

9. Problems with delivery

  • Marked delivered but not received: check with neighbours and your courier’s safe-drop notes, then email us within 5 working days. We’ll open a carrier trace and replace at our cost while it’s investigated.
  • Damaged in transit: photograph the box and contents before opening further, email the photos with your order number, and we’ll dispatch a replacement same-day.
  • Wrong address entered: if you spot a typo before dispatch (most orders), call us immediately on 027 474 0115 and we’ll fix it before the courier picks up.
