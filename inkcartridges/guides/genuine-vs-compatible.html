Genuine vs Compatible Ink Cartridges: What's the Difference?

If you own a printer in New Zealand, you have almost certainly faced this question: should you buy genuine ink cartridges from the original manufacturer, or go with a compatible alternative at a fraction of the price? Both options have their place, and the right choice depends on your printing needs, budget, and personal preference. In this guide we break down exactly what each type offers, address common concerns about quality and warranties, and help you make a confident, informed decision.

What Are Genuine Ink Cartridges?

Genuine ink cartridges — sometimes called OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) cartridges — are produced by the same company that made your printer. If you own an HP printer, the genuine cartridge is made by HP. Canon printers use Canon cartridges, Epson printers use Epson cartridges, and so on.

Because they are designed and tested alongside the printer hardware itself, genuine cartridges guarantee seamless compatibility and predictable print results. They are the benchmark for colour accuracy and are often the preferred choice for professional photography and design work where precise colour matching is essential. The trade-off is a premium price tag — genuine cartridges are typically the most expensive option on the shelf.

What Are Compatible Ink Cartridges?

Compatible ink cartridges are manufactured by specialist third-party companies and engineered to work in the same printers as their genuine counterparts. They are brand-new products — not refilled or remanufactured — built to meet international ISO/IEC 19752 and 19798 testing standards for page yield and print quality.

The biggest advantage is cost: compatible cartridges typically sell for 60–70% less than genuine equivalents, making them the go-to choice for homes, students, and businesses that print regularly and want to keep expenses under control. Modern compatibles deliver excellent print quality that most users find indistinguishable from genuine output in everyday documents and photos.

Will Compatible Cartridges Void My Warranty?

This is one of the most common concerns — and the short answer is no. Under the New Zealand Consumer Guarantees Act 1993, a printer manufacturer cannot void your warranty simply because you chose to use a third-party cartridge. Your warranty can only be affected if the cartridge itself directly causes a fault in the printer, which is exceptionally rare with quality-tested compatible products. In other words, you are legally protected when you choose compatible cartridges in New Zealand.

How to Choose the Right Cartridge

There is no single right answer — it depends on what you print and how often. If you are producing professional portfolios or archival photographs where exact colour fidelity matters, genuine cartridges give you the manufacturer's colour profile. For everything else — office documents, school assignments, everyday photo prints, shipping labels — compatible cartridges deliver outstanding results at a fraction of the cost. Many of our customers use genuine for colour-critical work and compatible for general printing, getting the best of both worlds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are compatible ink cartridges as good as genuine?

Yes. Modern compatible cartridges are manufactured to ISO/IEC 19752 and 19798 standards, delivering print quality and page yields that match or closely rival genuine OEM cartridges. At InkCartridges.co.nz, every compatible cartridge is backed by our quality guarantee.

Will using compatible cartridges void my printer warranty in New Zealand?

No. Under the New Zealand Consumer Guarantees Act 1993, a manufacturer cannot void your printer warranty simply because you used a third-party cartridge. The warranty can only be affected if the cartridge itself directly causes damage to the printer, which is extremely rare with quality compatibles.

How much can I save with compatible cartridges?

Compatible ink cartridges typically cost 60–70% less than their genuine OEM equivalents. For a household printing several hundred pages per month, this can translate to savings of hundreds of dollars each year.

Do compatible cartridges work with all printer brands?
