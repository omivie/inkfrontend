Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between ink cartridges and toner cartridges?

Ink cartridges contain liquid ink and are used in inkjet printers, while toner cartridges contain a fine powder and are used in laser printers. Toner cartridges generally have a much higher page yield, making them more cost-effective for high-volume printing. Laser printers with toner also tend to print faster and produce sharper text documents.

How long do toner cartridges last?

Toner cartridge lifespan depends on the model and your print volume. Standard toner cartridges typically yield 1,500 to 3,000 pages, while high-yield cartridges can produce 5,000 to 10,000 pages or more. Unlike ink, toner powder does not dry out, so unopened toner cartridges can be stored for several years without losing quality.

Are compatible toner cartridges as good as genuine?

High-quality compatible toner cartridges are engineered to meet or closely match the print quality and page yield of genuine OEM cartridges. Our compatible toner products undergo rigorous quality testing to ensure consistent, professional results. They offer significant cost savings, often 30-60% less than OEM equivalents, without compromising on output quality.
